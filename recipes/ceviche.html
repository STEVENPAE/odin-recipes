How to make Peruvian Ceviche

Description

The very known "Ceviche" is the most famous dish in Peru and we'll teach you how to make at home!

Ingredients

  • 1 large stalk celery, coarsely chopped.
  • ½ cup chopped white onion.
  • 1 ½. kosher salt, plus more.
  • 1 ½. peeled grated ginger.
  • ¼. grated garlic.
  • 1 thick white fish fillet, bloodline removed, 1 ½ oz. Flesh reseved for marinade.
  • 2 oz. fresh lime juice
  • 1 small Japanese sweet potato or small sweet potato, peeled, sliced ¼" thick Extra-virgin olive oil, for drizzling
  • ¼ cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 ¼ tsp. finely chopped cilantro leaves with tender stems, patted dry.
  • 1 aji limo chile, seeds removed, finely chopped.
  • Chancha, for serving.

Steps

  1. In a blender, blend celery, white onion, salt, ginger, garlic, 1 ½ oz. fish flesh, 1 cup ice, and ¼ cup water until mostly smooth. Transfer to a large bowl or pitcher. Add lime juice; season with salt, if needed. Refrigerate until chilled, about 1 hour. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve into a medium bowl; discard solids.
  2. Meanwhile, heat broiler to high. On a sheet pan, arrange potatoes in a single layer. Drizzle with oil, season with a pinch of salt, and toss to combine. Broil, watching closely, until fork-tender, 5 to 8 minutes. Let cool, then cut into quarters.
  3. Rinse red onion in a strainer under running water about 1 minute. Taste one slice. If onion still has a strong bite, continue to rinse until onion tastes sweet.
  4. Fill a large bowl with ice. Cut remaining fish into cubes or sashimi-like cuts. Place in prepared ice bowl, season with a pinch of salt, and mix with a spoon. Add red onion, potatoes, cilantro, and chile and toss to combine. Drizzle with 1 cup leche de tigre and toss again to combine.
  5. Arrange ceviche on chilled plate. Pour remaining leche de tigre over. Garnish with cancha.
